What the SGM must decide

Six resolutions — each addressing a specific failure

These are not abstract demands. Each resolution addresses a documented, specific failure of governance.

01
No confidence in the President's handling of the nominations

A recorded member vote on whether the President acted properly when the General Committee overruled the independent Nominations Committee's recommendations.

02
Resignation of committee members whose nominations were improperly reinstated

Committee members whose nominations were rejected by the Nominations Committee but reinstated by the General Committee are asked to resign.

03
Fresh elections under a fair, constitutionally compliant process

New elections under a process that respects the Constitution, the Nominations Committee, and the eligibility requirements that members have voted to introduce.

04
Independent reviews of the member expulsion and all 27 staff departures

An independent body to review the circumstances of the recent member expulsion and all 27 staff departures, reporting its findings to the membership.

05
Formation of a Constitutional Committee to verify and consolidate the Club's rules

A formal Constitutional Committee — with independent legal counsel — to produce a single verified Rule Book incorporating all changes since January 2016.

06
Legal costs from the 2025–2026 proceedings not to be borne by the Club

Legal costs from the 2025–2026 court proceedings not to be paid from Club funds unless formally authorised by members at a General Meeting.

Seven reforms passed by members — AGM, 28 March 2026. In force.
The SGM builds on these. Formal incorporation into the Constitution is planned for 30 September 2026.
  • GC minutes published to all members within 30 days
  • No two family members on the same committee simultaneously
  • Term limits: maximum two consecutive years in any one position
  • President & VP: 10 years' membership required · General Secretary: 5 years
  • Member expulsion only via independent five-person Disciplinary Committee
  • Same independent process applies to member suspension
  • Membership applications displayed publicly with applicant, proposer and seconder
