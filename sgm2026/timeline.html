The full record

A campaign built on documented patience

The petitioners did not rush to conflict. They wrote formally before the petition began. They submitted the petition through proper constitutional channels. They followed up. Three communications — each acknowledged, none acted on. The timeline below is the record.

28 March
Annual General Meeting
Contentious proceedings. Seven governance reforms passed by members. Nominations Committee recommendations overruled by the General Committee. Members not permitted to vote on the nominations matter.
2 April
Good-faith letter to the committee — before the petition began
Romesh Elapata and Sheran Fernando wrote formally to the committee setting out all points requiring resolution and inviting them to act — and stated directly that signatures of active full members were being collected. This was a direct opportunity to act before formal submission. It was not taken.
2 April
Petition opened — the call for signatures
With no meaningful response from the committee, members were invited to sign the petition. More than 70 agreed — well above the 50 required — and many more were ready to sign.
11 April
Petition formally submitted
More than 70 signed signatures — well above the 50 required — were handed to the General Secretary, Ms Shamalie Fonseka, by Sheran Fernando (Member 10317) on behalf of the petitioners. Many more were ready to sign at the time of submission. The petition met every constitutional requirement. The committee's obligation was triggered from that moment.
Post-11 April
The committee stalls — letters acknowledged, none acted on
Further written communications were sent to the committee — each clear, patient, and offering every reasonable accommodation. The committee acknowledged receipt of the petition but has not set a date. Acknowledgement without action is not compliance. The petition remains open.
By 2 May
16 May SGM — this window has closed
Notice by 2 May would have allowed an SGM on Saturday 16 May. The committee did not act. That window has now closed.
By 9 May
Notice for a 23 May SGM — the only acceptable date
Notice by 9 May allows the SGM on Saturday 23 May. This is now the only date the petitioners will accept. Every day of continued silence is a day of contempt for the members who have signed and supported this petition.
Beyond May
No longer acceptable — all available courses of action
Any date beyond 23 May is not acceptable to the petitioners. Three communications were sent — each acknowledged, none acted on. Every avenue of good faith has been exhausted. If the committee does not give notice by 9 May, the petitioners will have no alternative but to pursue all available courses of action.
