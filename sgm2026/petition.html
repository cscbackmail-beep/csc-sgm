The petition

What it asks for — and who submitted it

The petition was signed by more than 70 eligible voting members of the Colombo Swimming Club — well above the 50 required by the Constitution — and submitted formally to the General Secretary, Ms Shamalie Fonseka, on 11 April 2026. Many more were ready to sign at the time of submission, and support has continued to grow since. The petition asks the General Committee to call a Special General Meeting of the full membership to consider six resolutions concerning the governance of the Club.

The constitutional obligation

What the Club's own rules require

The CSC Constitution is unambiguous. When a valid petition is received from sufficient eligible members, the General Committee must call a Special General Meeting. This is not a discretionary power — it is an obligation.

Requirements the petition has met
  • Signed by more than 50 eligible voting members of the Club
  • Members who signed hold current, valid and active memberships
  • Submitted in writing to the General Secretary
  • Sets out the specific resolutions to be considered at the meeting
  • Delivered in person — the General Secretary has confirmed receipt

The constitutional basis for the petition is Rule 28 of the Club's Constitution and Rules. Rule 28 also requires that all members receive at least 14 days' notice before any General Meeting is held. The committee has had the petition since 11 April — 20 days with no date set. The only remaining acceptable date is 23 May (notice required by 9 May). A proposed amendment to the Constitution would add an explicit deadline for the committee to call the SGM after receiving a valid petition — closing the loophole the committee is currently exploiting. This amendment is among those proposed for the constitutional SGM of 30 September 2026.

Current status

The committee is stalling — we are building pressure

Three communications — acknowledged, none acted on

On 2 April the committee received a formal letter from Romesh Elapata and Sheran Fernando setting out all points requiring resolution — and was told directly that signatures of active full members were being collected. This was a direct opportunity to act before formal submission. With no meaningful response, the petition arrived on 11 April with 70+ signatures. Follow-up letters since. Each communication was acknowledged — none resulted in action. No date has been set. Only 23 May (notice required by 9 May) is now acceptable. If the committee does not give notice by 9 May, the petitioners will have no alternative but to pursue all available courses of action.

The petition remains open. Active members who have not yet added their name are encouraged to do so. Share this site with fellow members — many in the diaspora are unaware of what is happening. Every additional signature makes the committee's position harder to hold.
